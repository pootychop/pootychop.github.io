Julianna Beckert

Art + Performance Bio

I’m Julianna. I am a warm-hearted and enthusiastic contributor to team dynamics. I first caught the performance bug at art school (Kansas City Art Institute). I majored in Sculpture and focused on installation art. My final art project included motors and cams that made foam-filled pantyhose dance to a soundtrack of big-band/latin music.

Some other sculpture majors and I founded a performance art cheerleading squad with a lasting legacy — Rah-Booty! We made our own costumes, wrote and performed cheers about femininity, politics, social norms, and UTIs. We eventually started creating music as well. I collected toy instruments and soldered in jacks so we could plug them in for shows and recordings.

I’ve collaborated on a whole bunch of musical projects over the years, been in bands, danced in a couple different music videos, and even went on tour with Ssion as a dancer. I can program a drum machine, play the ukulele, I play an old synth keyboard and I sing.

My friend Leone and I wrote and produced a piece that echoed the American dream with a trip down Route 66. We acted an experience of life from birth, through death and into the afterlife with references to American culture and media. We called it Universe Women.

More recently I’ve been doing tons of improv at Voodoo Comedy and I’m looking for more artful experiences to perform with.
